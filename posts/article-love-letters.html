The Art of Love Letters

In a digital age of texts and DMs, a handwritten love letter stands out as a powerful gesture of affection.

Why Write a Letter?

A letter is tangible. It's something your partner can hold, keep, and reread years later. It requires time and thought, which makes it inherently valuable.

How to Start

Don't worry about being a poet. Start simply. "I was thinking about you and..." is a perfect opening. Be specific about what you appreciate about them.

Focus on Feelings, Not Just Facts

Instead of listing things you did ("We went to dinner"), describe how you felt ("I loved watching you laugh at dinner"). Vulnerability is the key to a great love letter.

The Delivery

Leave it on their pillow, hide it in their bag, or mail it—even if you live together! The surprise of finding it adds to the romance.

"Letters are the only way to safeguard the permanence of love."
